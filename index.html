The real finding

It isn't an awareness problem.
It's a consistency problem.

Most research on this topic stops at "social media harms mental health" — something nearly everyone already accepts. Our study went further and asked a sharper question: of the people who know they're affected and actually try to do something about it, how many succeed?

74.7%
said social media has jeopardized their studies, finances, relationships, or career — definitely or "maybe"
60.4%
have actively tried to quit or reduce their social media use at least once
31.6%
of those who tried did so consistently ("often" or "always") rather than just once or twice

In other words: awareness is common. Action is common. Follow-through is rare. That's the gap Detoxa measures — and the gap our self-assessment helps you locate yourself within.
